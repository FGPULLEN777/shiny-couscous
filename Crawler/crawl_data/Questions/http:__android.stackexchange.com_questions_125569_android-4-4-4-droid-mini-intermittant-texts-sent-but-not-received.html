Android 4.4.4, Droid Mini, intermittant texts sent but not received

up vote 0 down vote favorite
1

On Verizon Wireless with a Motorola Droid Mini, running Android 4.4.4. Stock Os, not rooted. All updates applied as offered by Carrier or Google Play Store.

Noticed over the past month or two that intermittent SMS messages appear to be sent from my phone, but are never received by the recipient. (usually found out in some inconvenient fashion.) There is nothing on my end to indicate that the message had any sending or delivery trouble. This has occurred sending to multiple people on different carriers. It has also occurred in the middle of a back and forth conversation, where 10-20 messages have been sent, but 1 or 2 don't get delivered. This only appears to happen with SMS, not MMS messages.

Also, on the My verizon web page, the un-delivered messages do show up as sent messages in the phone usage log. This seems to indicate that the message hit their network, and in the log it shows the correct From/To phone numbers. but I can't be sure that something about my phone isn't causing the message to be mis-addressed or something.

I have recently turned on SMS delivery reports hoping it would at least let me know when something got through. However, it seems some networks don't support SMS reports, which limits this feature. (I've only successfully gotten them from other VZW network or VZW MVNO phones)

Finally, and this may be a red herring, every SMS in my history shows the following in message details: "Cause Code: Addr.Vcnt., 0"

Some googling shows that to be a message that popped up in the past when people had SMS sending issues. Particularly back in Android Gingerbread days when there seems to have been a prevalent SMS bug. But the error message would often have popped up at them when sending.

Can anyone shed light on the meaning and potential relevance of that 'Cause Code', and suggestions for troubleshooting? So far VZW has suggesting rebooting my phone, and hasn't admitted to it possibly being the fault of their network.
